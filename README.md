**BANK MARKETING CAMPAIGN ANALYSIS**

**Project Overview**
This project analyze a real-world bank marketing dataset to evaluate campaign effectiveness and identify opportunities to improve customer targeting and conversion rates. The analysis focuses on customer segmentation, contact strategy, and historical campaign outcomes to support data-driven decision-making.

**Business Problem**
The bank experiences low conversion rates in outbound campaign for term deposits, resulting in high operational costs and inefficient outreach. The goal is to identify high-value customer segments and optimize campaign execution.

**Dataset** 
Source: UCI Machine Learning Repository - Bank Marketing Dataset
-> 45, 211 Customer records
-> 17 Attributes including demographics, campaign details, and outcomes

**Tools Used**
-> Python(Pandas)
-> Jupyter Notebook
-> SQL(for KPI logic)
-> Power BI(Dashboard - optional extension)

**Key Questions Answered**
-> How effective are repeated contact attempts ?
-> Which customer segments convert best?
-> Which contact methods yield higher success?
-> Does prior campaign outcome influence future conversion?

**Key Insights**
-> Overall conversion rate is ~11.7%
-> First contact performs best; repeated contacts reduce success
-> Students and retired customers convert 2-3x higher than average
-> Cellular outreach outperforms other contact method
-> Previous successful customer show ~65% conversion rate

**Recommendations**
-> Prioritize customers with prior successful outcomes.
-> Limit outreach to a maximum of 3 attempts
-> Focus campaigns on high-conversion segments 
-> Improve data quality before execution.

**Outcome**
This project demonstrates practical data analysis skills, business insight generation, and recommendation development aligned with Data Analyst and Business Analyst roles.
